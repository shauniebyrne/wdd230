Town of Taber

Taber was founded in 1907 by Mormon settlers and started as a coal mining town. Before that, it was known as Tank 77.

Corn Capital

Taber is known as the corn capital of the west because of the large amounts of sunshine the area receives.

Farming

Farmers grow more than corn. They grow potatoes, sugar beets, peas, and many other things.

Factories

Three main factories that provide jobs for the town are the sugar beet factory, and the two potato factories, Lamb Weston and McCains.

Cornfest

Cornfest is the largest free family festival in western Canada that is put on every year at the end of August.

Restaurants in Taber

There are many fast food and sit in restaurants available to eat at in Taber, including A&W, McDonalds, and Subway.

Grocery Stores

There are many grocery store options in Taber. These choices include: Walmart, Safeway, and Taber IGA.

Swimming Pool

Taber's swimming pool has a wade pool, a spray pool inlcuding lazy river, a hot tub, a diving pool, and a waterslide.

Splash Pad

During the summer, Taber's Confederation Park has a splash pad and a skate park.
